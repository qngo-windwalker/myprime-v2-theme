Description

People making high-risk choices often don't see that their problems are caused by their alcohol and drug use. The Spiral helps to explain why. It describes a collection of experiences that can trick someone into believing that their health and impairment problems are not associated with their high-risk alcohol and drug use.

These experiences include:

  • Social dependence that normalizes problems.
  • Enabling that removes consequences of problems.
  • Withdrawal learning, that teaches people that alcohol or drugs cure the pain of withdrawal.
  • Memory blackouts that erase people's recollection of consequences.
  • State-dependent learning that locks up emotions and skills.
  • Psychological defenses and mechanisms that make it more difficult to understand consequences.
  • Impaired abstract thinking skills that distort cause and effect.

The separate experiences of the Spiral in turn feed the Spiral, and that can propel our progression through the high-risk phases of use.
